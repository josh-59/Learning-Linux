Why Linux Mint

Under Construction!

The purpose of an operating system is to maintain persistent user data (files) and facilitate the execution of user programs on that data. With this in mind, the relative merits of an arbitrary operating system can be discussed objectively. This paper will focus on operating systems within the context of desktop use, though much of the discussion applies to other environments.

Ease of use is a very large topic, and is also enormously subjective. We will define "ease of use" to mean the least amount of learning required to use the product well. Again, the domain of this paper is desktop operating systems.
