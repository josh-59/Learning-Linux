About

Welcome to Learning Linux!

This began as a school project, and was originally called "Need to Know." The first chapter written was Text Terminals: A Historical Perspective, a re-imaging of David Lawyer's Text Terminal HOWTO, a Linux Documentation Project How-To. The intent was to distill its information to that relevant to modern GNU/Linux users, and also, to solve the "mysteries" of terminal emulation, as they appeared to me then.

Since then, this work has evolved into an attempt to concisely yet rigorously introduce the persistent concepts of GNU/Linux, as they apply to the desktop user, today.

Conventions:

  • Links are restricted to those that are both useful and non-obvious
    • Wikipedia is considered authoritative (and often, its documentation proves quite good)
  • Superscript references are used to reference authoritative sources that aren't necessarily of interest
    • i.e., don't read them, unless you must
  • Inline links and See Also's are used to point out immediately relevant sources
    • i.e., they're worth looking into

In keeping with both its origins in the Linux Documentation Project and its subject matter, this work is released under the Creative Commons Attribute-Sharealike 4.0 International License. Finally, while the emphasis is on Free and Open Source, non-free sources have been linked where merited.
